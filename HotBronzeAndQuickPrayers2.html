Polonium for Breakfast


Hot Bronze and Quick Prayers

Chapter 2

So, this is The Bad Neighbourhood. I really feel like I have a much better understanding of it now that I've made a single step over some arbitrary border. Everything looks... Uh... Exactly the same as it did one step ago. But now that I'm technically on the other side, it also feels more dangerous, more sinister. I'm one step away from perfect safety. The moment I crossed that godsforsaken line, I triggered a metaphorical tripwire. A cheap booby trap made of an empty can of beans will surely blow my legs off now. There's barely anyone here, but I there are still to many eyes are on me, like I'm a poorly thought out mascot meant to sell contacts.

But I've come this far. No point turning back now. I take another step. Then another. And another. With each step, I walk deeper into this horrifying place. It's terrible, but I don't stop. This is the only way. My hopes and dreams lie ahead, and they look suspiciously like dilapidated buildings and piss stained... Well, everything. I'm practically wading through a sea of cigarette butts and discarded needles.

The locals seem particularly fond of corners. Figures. They keep reminding me that I'm in the wrong neighbourhood. How thoughtful of them. I suppose my costume isn't all that convincing. I light one of the cigs to make myself look more menacing.

Gods know how many casinos and brothels I pass before I get to some seedy looking pub. The armed guards at the entrance really scream "reputable establishment". I rattle off a few prayers to what can only very generously be described as gods and enter.

I can't see my hand in front of my face through the thick tobacco smoke, and the dim lighting doesn't help. They probably think it hides the cokroaches. It doesn't. There's a giant spider fighting ring because of course there is. I also see folks getting stabbed left and right, hopefully for a good reason. I'd hate to lose my spleen because I looked at someone the wrong way. I'm just here to watch for now, learn about this place.

I take a seat and enjoy the wonderful lack of view. I don't get to relax in the cosy atmosphere for long, though. I'm told to order something or leave. They've got all sorts of drinks, from beer to antifreeze and motor oil, but I don't drink on the "job". Thankfully, they still sell coke, so I order that. They make me pay upfront, the paranoid bastards. I try to start a conversation with the not-so-slightly shitfaced guy sitting next to me.

"'Scuse me."

"The ffffuck do you wwwwwant?"

"I was wondering if you could tell me where I could buy a spleen and-"

"You aaaa... Ugh... Aaa... Yooaaah cop?"

"No, I was just-"

"Ffffuck... off!"

"I can pay if-"

"I ssshaid fughckghof!"

Well, that was productive. I look around to see if anyone looks like they might be talkative. Almost everyone's armed, and I'm getting some mean looks. Maybe asking potential gangsters questions isn't all that smart. I doubt Ouchara, Goddess of Stubbed Toes, will do much to protect me if I piss someone off. I think I'll just find stuff out some other way.

I leave the pub, my coke unsnorted. There's still a worrying number of eyes on me. Guess they can sense I don't belong here. I've got to find a way to fit in better. Perhaps if I cackle? That's evil, right? Eh, maybe later. For now, I think I'll just explore this place and hope I don't get murdered. This is going so much worse than planned. I really should have thought this through. But whatever. You live (hopefully) and you learn.

There's a lot of traffic going in and out of some large building. Looks promising. I can't quite make out what the sign says through the trees, so I get a bit closer aaaand... A-HA! Bingo! Just what I was looking for. The Black Supermarket. There are no guards at the door, but I doubt the place is unprotected. I say my prayers again and enter.

It's surprisingly clean inside. It would look a lot like a regular supermarket if it didn't use blacklight for the lighting. As it is, it almost feels like a night club. It's surprisingly clean and well supplied. They sell all sorts of goods, from guns to depleted uranium. And oh, black tar heroin is on sale. Hm. Black. Tar. That raises some questions about the contents of my cigarettes, but whatever. I go to the freezer section and find all sorts of exotic meats there. I'm pretty sure half of those are from endangered animals. There are also some human organs. I really wish I knew where they got those, but maybe it's for better that I don't. Whoever's responsible is probably too dangerous for me to handle.

The place is huge. Gods, there's even a small bakery at the back. They sell perfectly normal looking bread and pot brownies. I don't trust that. I bet the bread has razors or poison in it. Nothing here can be that innocent. A lot of the items in this place actually have proper packaging with fancy designs and text on the back. Too bad it's hard to read under this light, and it never lists the supplier or anything useful. It's another dead end as far as information goes. Learning about criminals isn't easy, who'd have thought? Maybe I could ask one of the cashiers if they know anything. As I approach them I notice they have snacks and sweets next to the cash register, just like in a regular superma- Aaaaaaand they're all drugs. Because of course they are. You know what? Fuck this place, I'm sick of this shit, I'm going home. The hero of Ipswich needs a break and a new plan.
